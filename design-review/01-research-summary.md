## Common premium patterns observed

1. **The work carries the luxury, not the UI.** Every top-tier site keeps chrome near-monochrome and minimal so high-resolution, professionally-shot project photography dominates. Colour comes from the photographs (velvet, marble, brass), never from decorative UI.
2. **Cinematic, templated project pages.** David Collins and TEN use an almost identical skeleton: small meta line (location + year), a one-line evocative intro, an uninterrupted vertical stack of full-bleed images, broken only by one centred pull-quote. Standardise it so *every job becomes a named case study*.
3. **Hard facts, then a sensory story.** TEN leads each project with a `Client / Location / Scope` metadata block, then an "Our inspiration" paragraph on materials and feeling. Directly transferable to Propertycare transformations.
4. **Extreme whitespace + constrained measure.** Content capped ~1080–1160px, text columns clamped ~640–700px, generous vertical section padding so each block "breathes." The single most transferable premium cue.
5. **Disciplined type system.** One distinctive display family (editorial serif or a confident grotesque) + one clean sans for body; hierarchy built from *weight and whitespace*, not loud colour. Oversized headline states the positioning; one large italic/serif pull-quote per project is the emotional centrepiece.
6. **Named, dated, curated collections.** Work titled like a magazine ("Calibre 2025", period/place-based names), often explicitly *limited* ("a sampling of our most memorable projects") — scarcity reads as exclusivity.
7. **Branded, numbered process modules.** Stokesman's Discover/Design/Define/Development/Delivery, Cornerstone's design-build process — turns an enquiry into an educated, confident lead.
8. **Stacked, verifiable trust.** Accreditation badge walls (ARB/RIBA/SBID for designers → NICEIC/Part P/TrustMark/Dulux Select for trades), named photo-attributed testimonials tying design to outcome, live review widgets, real team bios with credentials, dated "recent work" feeds proving the firm is active.
9. **One dominant, low-friction CTA repeated everywhere.** Beams repeats a single "Get your estimate" pill at every scroll depth with a "2 mins / no obligation / free" reassurance; Fusion adds a floating WhatsApp widget with pre-filled messages.
10. **Restrained, comprehension-first motion.** Lenis-style smooth scroll with the long image stack as the primary motion device; one signature hero moment (before/after wipe or muted ambient video), soft fade-ins, staggered reveals — never spectacle.
11. **Serious image delivery.** Focal-point responsive CDN pipelines (auto AVIF/WebP, fixed display widths, lazy-load + soft fade). Crisp, fast, art-directed photos are non-negotiable for perceived quality.
12. **B2B / partner funnel alongside the homeowner funnel.** Haven's "For Architects", sector pages — route trade/referral partners (landlords, agents, developers, designers) separately.
13. **Content + FAQ authority layer.** Expert insights and FAQ accordions pre-empt objections (cost, duration, insurance) and build search authority.

## Best practices to adopt for Propertycare

- Treat finished media walls, paint finishes and electrical installs as **gallery photography on the dark canvas** — let the craft be the colour.
- Build the **cinematic transformation template** (brass meta-line → title → one-line intro → before/after slider → full-bleed image stack → one pull-quote → "Discover more").
- Add a **drag-wipe before/after slider** as the signature interaction (paired stills as mobile/no-JS fallback) — the literal embodiment of "Transformations."
- **Name and date** every transformation with a real location/postcode (doubles as local SEO).
- Repeat **one calm primary verb** ("Book a free survey" / "Tell us about your space") at every scroll depth, with friction-killers.
- Build a **recognisable trust bar** (NICEIC/Part P, TrustMark, Dulux Select, fully insured, written guarantee) repeated top and bottom.
- Ship a **numbered, branded process** module with brass numerals and slow scroll-reveal.

## Common mistakes to avoid (from the exemplars + competitor scan)

- Generic stock photography for "completed work" (undercuts the "we actually did this" proof).
- Walls of all-caps text and long undifferentiated scrolls.
- Pushy popups and aggressive multi-CTA confusion.
- Heavy parallax / WebGL / autoplay-with-sound — motion that delays content or hurts Core Web Vitals.
- Brass/gold used loudly enough to read as kitsch (keep metallic *sparing*).

> **Guiding principle distilled:** *Quiet, confident luxury — let real before/after photography and editorial restraint do the selling, with one green action colour and one sparing brass prestige accent.*
